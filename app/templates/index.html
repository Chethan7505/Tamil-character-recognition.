About the Project

SiRi's is an end-to-end deep learning system for recognising handwritten Tamil script — one of the world's oldest classical languages. Tamil has a complex alphabet of 247 characters. Our model covers the 156 most commonly written characters, including vowels (uyir), consonants (mei), and compound characters (uyir-mei).

The system accepts a drawn stroke or a real photograph, applies centre-of-mass normalisation, thickens the strokes to match training conditions, and feeds the result through a deep CNN to output a confident prediction in milliseconds.

Development Process

1
Dataset Preparation

Downloaded the HP Labs India handwritten Tamil dataset. Preprocessed 500K+ images into 156 class folders, normalised to 64×64 grayscale using centre-of-mass alignment and stroke thickening (MaxFilter).

2
CNN Architecture Design

Designed a custom 6-layer CNN: three Conv→BN→ReLU→MaxPool blocks with increasing depth (16→32→64 filters), followed by fully-connected layers of 1024→512→156 neurons with BatchNorm regularisation.

3
Training on Google Colab (GPU)

Trained for 30 epochs using a free T4 GPU on Google Colab. Accuracy climbed from 74% in Epoch 1 to 92.2% validation accuracy by Epoch 30. Model saved as tamil_net.pt.

4
Flask Web Application

Built a Python Flask backend. The canvas drawing is sent as a base64 PNG and decoded server-side. Added a second /predict_upload route to handle real photo uploads through the same preprocessing pipeline.

5
Image Recognition Feature

Extended the app to recognise real handwriting photos. The pipeline automatically inverts dark-on-white photos, crops the character, thickens strokes, and centre-aligns to 64×64 — identical to training data format.
